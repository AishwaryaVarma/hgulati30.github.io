About Me

Hey! I am a current graduate student at Stony Brook University. I am a foresightful, diligent and a highly regulated individual, and my interests broadly include Applied Machine Learning, and Natural Language Processing. Before joining SBU, I spent wonderful 6 months as a project intern at the Supercomputer and Education Research(SERC) in the Department of Computational and Data Sciences (CDS) at Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore. One aspect of my personality that I always strived to supplement with my work and academics is a love for community building. Having been acquainted with the Google Developer Groups (GDG), Bangalore for the past two years, I explored a different side of myself, functioning along with one of the most innovative work environment cultures. I helped evangelize technology and raised strong industry-standard skills by aiding in management at various junctures subsidized by Google, thereby featuring in a plethora of talks mainly focused on community building and learning about Google's technologies. On the account of rigorousness and the quest for improving my expertise, I was nominated to participate in Google's women's meetup powered by Google Maps to collaborate with the twenty Fellow Local Guides from Bangalore with an opportunity for designing safety feature prototypes for women.
